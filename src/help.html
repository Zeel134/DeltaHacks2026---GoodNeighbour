Help & Documentation

Welcome to Good Neighbor

Good Neighbor is a community-driven platform that connects people who need help with those willing to provide it. Our mission is to build stronger, more connected communities by making it easy for neighbors to help each other.

Getting Started

1. Create an Account

Sign up with a username and password. Your account allows you to both request help and offer assistance to others in your community.

2. Choose Your Action

After logging in, you'll see two main options:

  • I want help - Post a request for assistance with a task
  • I want to help - Browse and accept tasks from your neighbors

Requesting Help

When you need assistance, follow these steps:

  • Task Title: Provide a clear, descriptive title for your request
  • Category: Select the type of help you need (moving, gardening, tech support, etc.)
  • Duration: Estimate how long the task will take
  • Location: Enter your address or click on the map to set your location
  • Urgency: Indicate how soon you need help (High, Medium, or Low)

Once submitted, your request will be visible to other community members who can accept and help you.

Providing Help

Browse available tasks and use filters to find opportunities that match your availability:

Available Filters

  • Sort By: Urgency, Distance, Newest, or Oldest
  • Urgency Level: Filter by how urgent the task is
  • Distance: Show only tasks within a certain radius
  • Duration: Filter by estimated time commitment
  • Category: Select specific types of tasks you're interested in

Accepting Tasks

Click on any task to view details. If you'd like to help, click "Accept Task" to add it to your active tasks list.

Managing Your Tasks

Click the "My Active Tasks" button to view all tasks you've accepted. Once you've completed a task, mark it as complete to remove it from your active list.

Privacy & Safety

  • Your exact location is only shared when you post a help request
  • All data is stored locally in your browser
  • Always meet in public places when appropriate
  • Trust your instincts and prioritize your safety

Tips for Success

  • Be specific and clear in your task descriptions
  • Respond promptly to those who accept your requests
  • Show appreciation for help received
  • Start with smaller tasks to build trust in the community
  • Be reliable - if you accept a task, follow through

Technical Requirements

  • Modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
  • JavaScript enabled
  • Location services enabled for distance calculations (optional)
  • Internet connection for map features
